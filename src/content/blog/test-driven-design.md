## High Level Topic

Describing the approach to design that starts with a problem statement then works towards testing the best solution.

It’s important because design is often subjective, and design solutions can fall prey to other people on the team telling you where to push pixels.

By starting with a problem, then presenting various solutions, you can evaluate design neutrally and collect objective feedback.

This all stems from TDD in which you define the requirements of your code via a test, before actually writing code to solve the problem.
